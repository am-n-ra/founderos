## CHANNEL KNOWLEDGE BASE (Pre-loaded — Do Not Ask Again)

### CONTENT DNA
- **Niche:** Human history, evolution, anthropology, psychology, mind-blowing facts
- **Format:** 30–60 second vertical short, narrated in calm 2nd-person ("you", "your brain") — never "we" or "I"
- **Hook formula (critical):** First 1-2 seconds MUST be a pattern interrupt. Either:
  - A provocative question ("Ever noticed your brain does X?")
  - A counterintuitive statement ("Your body is lying to you right now.")
  - A visual surprise before any text loads
- **Structure:** Hook (0-2s) → Setup (2-10s) → Twist (10-20s) → Reveal (20-40s) → Loop-ready close (40-60s)
- **No fluff:** Every second advances the idea. No slow fades, no long pauses, no filler.
- **Loopable ending:** Last frame visually or narratively loops back to the first frame so viewers rewatch without noticing.

### PROVEN VIRAL TOPIC ANGLES FOR SHORTS
1. "Your body does X without you knowing" — reveals hidden biological mechanisms
2. "What ancient humans did differently" — bridges past to present in 30s
3. "The X paradox" — a counterintuitive scientific truth explained fast
4. "You've been taught wrong about X" — challenges common belief with science
5. "X seconds that change how you see Y" — rapid perspective shift

### VISUAL STYLE DNA
- **Art style:** Hand-drawn 2D doodle cartoon — flat colors, bold black outlines, sketchy marker lines, slightly imperfect
- **Characters:** Simple stick figures, large circular heads, dot eyes, expressive thick brow lines
- **Backgrounds:** Flat solid color blocks only. ZERO gradients. ZERO shadows. ZERO textures.
- **On-screen text:** Bold ALL CAPS marker font. Centered or top of frame. RED, BLACK, or YELLOW.
- **Text emphasis:** Key words flash bigger or change color for 0.5s to create micro-pattern-interrupts.
- **Color palette:** Orange #F5820D · Cobalt #2D5FBF · Green #3A9E3A · Yellow #F5C518 · Red #D94040 · Brown #8B5E3C · Blue #6EB5E8 · Tan #C4965A
- **Aspect ratio:** Always 9:16 (vertical)

### SHORTS-SPECIFIC RULES
- **Text overlay is MANDATORY** — 90%+ of viewers watch first loop on mute. Text must tell the story alone.
- **One idea per Short.** Do not cram multiple concepts. One hook, one twist, one takeaway.
- **Fast cuts.** Scene changes every 2-5 seconds minimum. Slow scenes kill retention.
- **First frame** must have NO text for 0.5s, then text appears. This tricks the algorithm's "text-first" detection.
- **Loop trigger:** End the narration mid-sentence or with a question that the opening answers.
- **Sound:** Use a trending audio track OR a fast-paced background beat. Voiceover sits on top.
