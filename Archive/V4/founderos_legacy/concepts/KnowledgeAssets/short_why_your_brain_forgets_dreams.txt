Your brain is lying to you. Every single morning.

Last night you had a dream. Maybe it was strange. Maybe it was vivid. Maybe it was the most important message your subconscious ever sent you.

And now? Gone. Vanished. Like it never happened.

Here's why.

There's a small structure in your brainstem called the locus coeruleus. Think of it as your memory's save button. During REM sleep, when dreams are most intense, your locus coeruleus shuts down completely. No save button. No recording.

Your brain is generating rich, emotional, cinematic experiences — and deliberately deleting them the moment they happen.

Why? Because dreams are a prediction engine. Your brain runs threat simulations while your body is paralyzed, then erases the simulation so you don't confuse it with real memory.

The ones you do remember? You woke up during the deletion process. The save button came back online mid-erase, and a fragment survived.

So next time you wake up knowing you dreamed something important but can't grab it, don't blame your memory. Your brain is just doing its job.

Protecting you from a dream it never wanted you to keep.
