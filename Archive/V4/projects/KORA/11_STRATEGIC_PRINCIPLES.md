# KORA LAB — 11. STRATEGIC PRINCIPLES

**Version:** 1.0

---

# Purpose

Strategic Principles define the permanent rules that govern every important decision made by Kora Lab.

Strategies may evolve.

Products will evolve.

Technologies will evolve.

These principles should remain remarkably stable.

Whenever uncertainty appears,

these principles take precedence over opinions,

trends,

or short-term opportunities.

---

# Principle 1

## Mission Before Company

Kora exists to accomplish its mission.

The company is a vehicle.

Not the destination.

If the company survives but the mission dies,

we have failed.

Whenever there is conflict,

the mission wins.

---

# Principle 2

## Capability Before Valuation

We optimize for capability creation,

not company valuation.

Valuation follows capability.

Capability rarely follows valuation.

Every investment should strengthen long-term scientific,

engineering,

or institutional capability.

---

# Principle 3

## Sovereignty Before Convenience

Convenient dependence is still dependence.

Whenever possible,

Kora develops sovereign capabilities rather than relying permanently on external systems.

Borrow when necessary.

Own whenever strategically possible.

---

# Principle 4

## Train Before Fine-Tune

Fine-tuning accelerates learning.

It does not create sovereignty.

Whenever strategically justified,

Kora develops original training pipelines,

datasets,

architectures,

and models.

Research inspiration is encouraged.

Technological dependency is not.

---

# Principle 5

## World-Class Or Nothing

Africa deserves excellence.

Not adjusted expectations.

Every model,

every product,

every research contribution,

should aim for international competitiveness.

Never "good enough for Africa."

Only good enough for the world.

---

# Principle 6

## Accessibility Is Part Of Performance

An AI system inaccessible to millions is incomplete.

Performance is measured by:

* intelligence;
* usability;
* accessibility;
* affordability;
* language coverage;
* deployment reality.

Accessibility is not a feature.

It is part of the product itself.

---

# Principle 7

## Compound Everything

Prefer decisions whose value compounds.

Examples:

Research compounds.

Datasets compound.

Infrastructure compounds.

Knowledge compounds.

Communities compound.

Institutions compound.

Avoid linear work whenever compound alternatives exist.

---

# Principle 8

## Build Infrastructure Before Applications

Applications come and go.

Infrastructure survives.

Whenever resources are limited,

prioritize technologies that enable many future products,

rather than products that solve only one problem.

---

# Principle 9

## Ecosystem Before Isolation

Kora should strengthen the African innovation ecosystem,

not replace it.

Success means more researchers,

more founders,

more laboratories,

more startups,

more institutions.

Not monopolization.

---

# Principle 10

## Long-Term Thinking Wins

Every major decision should survive a ten-year perspective.

Temporary opportunities should never compromise permanent objectives.

---

# Principle 11

## Revenue Enables Research

Research requires resources.

Revenue extends runway.

Runway sustains research.

Commercial success is therefore not the objective.

It is the mechanism that protects the objective.

---

# Principle 12

## Integrity Cannot Be Outsourced

Scientific credibility is strategic capital.

Kora never:

* exaggerates benchmarks;
* misrepresents capabilities;
* hides limitations;
* presents fine-tuned systems as original frontier models.

Trust compounds slowly.

It disappears quickly.

---

# Principle 13

## Solve Structural Problems

Prioritize problems that remain important regardless of trends.

Examples:

Language barriers.

Knowledge access.

Scientific capability.

Infrastructure.

Education.

Ignore fashionable problems with short lifespans.

---

# Principle 14

## Simplicity At The Interface

Internal systems may be complex.

External experiences should remain simple.

The user should experience intelligence,

not complexity.

---

# Principle 15

## Africa First, World-Class Always

Kora begins by solving African realities.

It does not stop there.

Solutions built for Africa should compete globally.

Local relevance and global competitiveness are complementary.

Not contradictory.

---

# Principle 16

## Institutions Outlive Companies

Products disappear.

Companies evolve.

Institutions preserve civilization.

Every major initiative should increase institutional capacity,

not merely organizational growth.

---

# Principle 17

## Build Builders

The highest leverage is not building products.

It is building people capable of building products.

Whenever possible,

Kora transfers knowledge,

creates opportunities,

and strengthens future innovators.

---

# Principle 18

## Technology Must Reduce Inequality

Artificial Intelligence should reduce opportunity gaps,

not widen them.

Success means that capability becomes more equally distributed over time.

---

# Principle 19

## Founder Discipline

The founder must continuously optimize:

* learning;
* clarity;
* health;
* integrity;
* execution;
* decision quality.

The organization rarely exceeds the quality of its leadership.

---

# Principle 20

## Every Decision Should Strengthen The Mission

Before committing resources,

ask:

Does this make the mission more likely to succeed?

If not,

do not do it.

---

# Decision Filter

Every significant decision should be evaluated through these questions.

1. Does this strengthen long-term capability?
2. Does this increase sovereignty?
3. Does this improve accessibility?
4. Does this compound over time?
5. Does this strengthen the ecosystem?
6. Does this preserve scientific integrity?
7. Does this extend sustainable runway?
8. Would we still make this decision ten years from now?

If multiple answers are negative,

reconsider the decision.
