# KORA LAB — 12. NORTH STAR

**Version:** 1.0

---

# Purpose

The North Star defines the single long-term strategic objective that aligns every decision made by Kora Lab.

Organizations fail when every team optimizes different metrics.

The North Star ensures that research,

engineering,

products,

finance,

operations,

and partnerships all move toward the same destination.

It is not a KPI.

It is the primary indicator that the mission is becoming reality.

---

# North Star Statement

**Increase Africa's Sovereign Artificial Intelligence Capability until importing frontier AI is no longer a strategic necessity.**

Everything Kora does should contribute to this objective.

---

# Why This North Star

Revenue can increase while dependence increases.

Users can increase while sovereignty decreases.

Funding can increase while research capability stagnates.

The mission is not to maximize business metrics.

The mission is to maximize African AI capability.

Therefore,

the North Star measures capability,

not popularity.

---

# What "Capability" Means

Capability is the ability to independently create,

improve,

deploy,

and sustain frontier AI systems.

Capability exists when Africa possesses:

* researchers;
* engineers;
* compute;
* datasets;
* infrastructure;
* institutions;
* products;
* adoption;
* continuous innovation.

The North Star measures the growth of this entire system.

---

# Strategic Dimensions

The North Star is evaluated through five permanent dimensions.

---

## Dimension 1

### Frontier Capability

Questions:

Can Kora train increasingly capable frontier models?

Can it publish original research?

Can it compete technically with leading laboratories?

Indicators include:

* benchmark performance;
* research output;
* training capability;
* model quality.

---

## Dimension 2

### Sovereignty

Questions:

How much strategic AI capability is owned rather than imported?

Indicators include:

* proprietary models;
* proprietary datasets;
* proprietary infrastructure;
* independent deployment capability.

---

## Dimension 3

### Accessibility

Questions:

Can ordinary Africans actually benefit from frontier intelligence?

Indicators include:

* African language support;
* voice interaction;
* affordability;
* offline deployment;
* accessibility across education levels.

---

## Dimension 4

### Ecosystem Growth

Questions:

Is Kora creating more builders?

Indicators include:

* startups created;
* researchers trained;
* developers using Kora;
* universities collaborating;
* ecosystem partnerships.

---

## Dimension 5

### Institutional Strength

Questions:

Would progress continue if today's leadership disappeared?

Indicators include:

* research programs;
* governance;
* talent pipeline;
* institutional partnerships;
* long-term sustainability.

---

# Success Definition

The North Star is achieved when:

African governments,

businesses,

developers,

researchers,

and citizens naturally choose African AI because it is among the world's best.

Not because it is local.

Not because it is subsidized.

Because it is excellent.

---

# Secondary Metrics

The North Star is supported—not replaced—by secondary metrics.

Examples include:

Financial:

* recurring revenue;
* runway;
* capital efficiency.

Research:

* publications;
* benchmarks;
* training runs.

Engineering:

* deployment reliability;
* inference latency;
* cost efficiency.

Products:

* active users;
* retention;
* engagement.

Accessibility:

* supported languages;
* voice usage;
* offline adoption.

Ecosystem:

* startups launched;
* partnerships;
* developers.

These metrics matter.

But only because they support the North Star.

---

# Metrics We Refuse To Optimize

Kora does not optimize primarily for:

* valuation;
* media coverage;
* social media followers;
* vanity benchmarks;
* fundraising totals;
* download counts without meaningful use.

These are outputs.

Not objectives.

---

# Decision Hierarchy

Every strategic decision follows this order.

Mission

↓

North Star

↓

Strategic Principles

↓

Projects

↓

Tasks

Whenever lower-level decisions conflict with higher-level objectives,

the higher level prevails.

---

# Time Horizon

The North Star should remain stable for at least twenty years.

Products may evolve.

Markets may evolve.

Technologies may evolve.

The direction should remain constant.

---

# Organizational Alignment

Every team should understand how its work contributes to the North Star.

Research increases frontier capability.

Engineering transforms capability into usable systems.

Products expand accessibility.

Operations increase sustainability.

Business generates runway.

Partnerships strengthen the ecosystem.

No department exists independently.

---

# Decision Filter

Before approving any initiative, ask:

1. Does this increase African AI capability?
2. Does this reduce technological dependence?
3. Does this strengthen long-term sovereignty?
4. Does this expand accessibility?
5. Does this reinforce the ecosystem?
6. Would losing this initiative slow the North Star?

If the answer is "No" to most questions,

the initiative is probably tactical,

not strategic.
