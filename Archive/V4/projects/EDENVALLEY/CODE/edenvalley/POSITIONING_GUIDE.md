# Eden Valley - Positioning Guide
## What Are We? (For Investors, Backers, and Team)

---

## THE QUESTION

> "Is this a combinator? A fund? An incubator? An OS?"

**Short answer:** We're a new category. Call it an **"AI-Powered Incubator with Passion-Based Matching + Crowdfunding Support."**

**Why it matters:** The category you choose determines who you're compared to, which determines how you're valued.

---

## CATEGORY OPTIONS ANALYZED

### Option 1: "We're a Combinator"

**Pros:**
- YC is the gold standard, everyone understands it
- Combinators take equity, so do we
- Clear business model reference

**Cons:**
- Sets wrong expectations (we're not YC)
- YC is batch-based, we're continuous
- YC has human mentors, we have AI
- YC is elite/selective, we're open/democratic

**Verdict:** Partial fit. Use as reference, not identity.

---

### Option 2: "We're a Fund"

**Pros:**
- We take equity and % of funding
- Fund-like returns if successful
- Appeals to financial investors

**Cons:**
- We don't invest cash (at least not initially)
- We provide infrastructure, not capital
- "Fund" implies LP structure, regulation
- Wrong expectations about returns timeline

**Verdict:** Not accurate. We enable funding, we don't provide it.

---

### Option 3: "We're an Incubator"

**Pros:**
- We incubate ventures from idea to funding
- Incubators provide structure (we do)
- Broad enough to include our features
- "AI-powered incubator" is unique

**Cons:**
- Old-school incubators had physical spaces
- Some incubators charge fees (we don't)
- Can sound "lightweight" vs accelerators

**Verdict:** Good fit. This is our core identity.

**Refined:** "AI-powered incubator with passion-based matching + crowdfunding support"

---

### Option 4: "We're an Operating System"

**Pros:**
- "OS" implies infrastructure, platform
- Tech-forward, scalable
- Recurring usage/revenue potential
- Matches our "moat" language

**Cons:**
- Abstract — needs explanation
- OS usually implies software only (we have community)
- May confuse with actual OS software
- Harder to explain to non-tech investors

**Verdict:** Good internal metaphor, confusing external positioning.

**Use internally:** "We're building the OS for startup formation"
**Use externally:** "AI-powered incubator"

---

### Option 5: "We're a Marketplace"

**Pros:**
- Two-sided (Thinkers + Doers)
- Network effects
- Transaction-based revenue potential

**Cons:**
- Marketplaces are usually for commodities
- Our value is the structure, not just matching
- "Marketplace" sounds lightweight

**Verdict:** Under-sells us. We're more than a marketplace.

---

## UNIQUE ATTRIBUTES (What Makes Us "Us")

### 1. The Thinker-Doer Framework
No one else has researched and operationalized this split. It's our IP.

### 2. Passion-Based Matching
Skills matter, but shared purpose matters more. No one else leads with passion.

### 3. Relationship Infrastructure
Everyone does matching. No one does post-match scaffolding (contracts, equity, governance).

### 4. AI at Every Layer
Pischon isn't just a chatbot. It's specialized agents for: blueprints, matching, contracts, execution, pitching.

### 5. Crowdfunding Integration
Built-in support for crowdfunders from day one, not just VCs later.

### 6. The Two Walls
Euphrates (team building) + Havila (funding showcase) = unique architecture.
