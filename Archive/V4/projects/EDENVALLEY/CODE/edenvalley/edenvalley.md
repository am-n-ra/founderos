## 2. The Problem: Executive Dysfunction

### What Is It?
**Executive dysfunction** is the inability to move from idea to concrete action. It's not laziness. It's neurological.

### The 5 ADHD Barriers
| Barrier | Symptom | Startup Impact |
|---------|---------|----------------|
| **Task Initiation** | Can't start despite motivation | Idea stays in head or notes |
| **Working Memory** | Forgets priority when switching | Loses strategic thread |
| **Dopamine Regulation** | Needs artificial urgency to act | Procrastinates until crisis |
| **Emotion Regulation** | Intense frustration paralyzes | Abandons at first obstacle |
| **Time Blindness** | Can't feel time passing | Missed deadlines, broken promises |

### The Key Stat
> **Entrepreneurs are 6x more likely to have ADHD** than the general population — but **80%** have professional difficulties related to executive dysfunction.

**The Cruel Paradox:** The same brain that generates genius ideas (hyperfocus, non-linear thinking, pattern detection) **cannot execute them alone**.

## 4. Team Structure

### Roles

**1. The Visionnaire (1 per startup)**
- Owner of the idea and strategic Blueprint
- Responsible for vision, innovation, product decisions
- Often neurodivergent (ADHD/Autism/Dyslexia) — creative brain needing external structure

**2. The Lead Doer (1 per startup)**
- **Co-founder at 42.5%** with Visionnaire (85% total for co-founders)
- Responsible for execution, operations, Blueprint implementation
- Makes operational decisions with Visionnaire (structured consensus)
- Only Doer with co-founder status and equal decision power

**3. Other Doers (0 to N per startup)**
- **NOT co-founders** — join as team members after Visionnaire-Lead Doer match
- **Dedicated equity pool: 10%** for all other Doers (distributed by contribution)
- Can be: developers, designers, marketers, operations, etc.
- Vesting over 2-4 years based on engagement (startup standard)
- Compensation: salary + equity from pool based on contribution
- Pischon coordinates and helps them integrate into the Blueprint

## 6. Business Model

### Revenue Per Startup

| Source | Amount | Detail |
|--------|--------|--------|
| **Eden Valley Equity** | **5%** of startup | Taken at incorporation, locked by smart contract |
| **Team Doers Pool** | **10%** of startup | For other Doers joining the team (2-4 year vesting) |
| **Funds Raised** | **1%** of funds raised | Per funding round |
| **of which crowdfunder return** | **20%** of that 1% | Returned to early crowdfunders who supported Eden Valley |

### Example
- Startup raises $1M → Eden Valley takes 1% = $10K
- 20% of $10K = $2K returned to crowdfunders
- Eden Valley keeps $8K + 5% equity in the startup
- Other Doers share the 10% pool based on their contribution

### Early Incorporation
Eden Valley helps **incorporate from first funds**:
- Optimized legal structure
- Smart contracts for equity
- Banking and accounting setup
- Anyone can invest in potential companies via the platform

### For Funders (Crowdfunders & VCs/Angels)
- **Crowdfunders:** Early access to structured startups, return on funds raised
- **VCs/Angels:** Qualified deal flow, pre-validated teams (Visionnaire + Lead Doer), Pischon-facilitated due diligence
