# The Doer Value Proposition
## Why Executors Will Join Eden Valley

---

## The Core Insight

From our research in `T&D_paradigm.md`, we found the **unclaimed vulnerability** for Doers:

> **"Doers are tired of being treated like replaceable labor while being blamed for decisions they never made."**

This is NOT about giving them ideas. It's about **power redistribution**.

---

## The Doer's Problem (Deep Dive)

### Current Reality

**What Doers Experience:**
1. "Just build it" — unclear direction
2. Blamed when it takes 3 months instead of 3 weeks
3. No voice in WHAT to build, only HOW
4. Treated as contractor, not co-founder
5. Technical expertise undervalued vs. "vision"

**The Research Says:**
- Engineers don't quit bad jobs — they quit environments with **no agency**
- 65% of startups fail due to co-founder conflict
- Technical founders get 30-50% valuation discounts if they're single points of failure

### The Psychological Pain

**Identity Crisis:**
- "I'm just the coder"
- "My contribution is invisible until something breaks"
- "The thinker gets the glory, I get the bugs"

**Power Imbalance:**
- Thinker makes decisions
- Doer executes decisions
- If product fails → Doer blamed for "bad execution"
- If product succeeds → Thinker praised for "great vision"

---

## Our Solution: Eden Valley for Doers

### The Promise

> **"Finally, a platform where thinkers can't proceed without your approval — and your expertise is treated as equal to their ideas."**

### What Changes

| Before Eden Valley | With Eden Valley |
|-------------------|------------------|
| Thinker has idea, hires Doer | Thinker posts blueprint, Doer **chooses** to apply |
| "Just build it" | Structured blueprint with clear requirements |
| Doer executes Thinker's vision | Doer contributes to **shared** vision |
| Blamed for timeline failures | Contracts define realistic expectations |
| No equity negotiation power | Standard equity frameworks protect Doers |
| Invisible contribution | Execution showcased on Havila wall |

### The Power Shifts

**1. Veto Power on Technical Decisions**
- Pischon AI enforces: "Doers have final say on technical architecture"
- Thinkers must get Doer approval on tech stack, build vs. buy, technical debt tradeoffs

**2. Shared Accountability**
- If product fails → both responsible (contractually defined)
- If product succeeds → both celebrated (Havila wall shows full team)

**3. Decision Authority, Not Just Execution Orders**
- Doers are co-founders from day one
- Equity agreements locked before building starts
- RACI framework gives Doers ownership of their domain

**4. Protection When Pushing Back**
- Pischon provides "pushback scripts" — how to challenge bad decisions
- Contracts protect Doers from scope creep
- Clear escalation paths for disagreements

**5. Visibility of Contribution**
- Havila wall showcases execution, not just vision
- GitHub integration shows code contribution
- Investors see technical architecture, not just pitch deck

## The Doer Journey on Eden Valley

### Step 1: Discovery (Havila Wall)
- Browse blueprints in your passion area
- See structured missions/goals (not just "I have an idea")
- Filter by: tech stack, equity terms, team size, industry

### Step 2: Application (Euphrates Wall)
- Apply with "how I'll contribute" (not just resume)
- Pischon helps Thinker ask the right technical questions
- Technical challenge / small project to validate fit

### Step 3: Selection
- Thinker chooses, but Doer must **also approve**
- Pischon generates: equity agreement, role definition, vesting schedule
- Both sign before any code is written

### Step 4: Building
- Integrated tools (GitHub, task management)
- Pischon provides clarity when Thinker unavailable
- KPIs tracked transparently
- Regular founder syncs (relationship check-ins)

### Step 5: Funding (Havila Wall)
- Execution showcased alongside vision
- Doer profile visible to investors
- Technical architecture part of pitch
- 1% of funding fee shared with platform (aligned incentives)
