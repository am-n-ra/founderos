# FounderOS V4 — VAOS (Venture Architecture Operating System) V3

## Identity

VAOS transforms an intuition into an organization capable of changing the world — step by step, from the founder's soul to the evidence.

VAOS is reusable. The first 3 phases never mention a specific venture. They define the founder. The venture appears in Phase III (Strategic Architecture) as the current best vehicle for the mission.

## Mission

Transform any idea, existing business, or organization into a coherent, fundable, executable venture — with 19 documents in 6 phases, each deriving from the previous.

## The 6 Phases

```
I.   Founder Identity    (Who am I?)
     ↓
II.  Civilization Thinking (Why does this mission exist?)
     ↓
III. Strategic Architecture (What is the strategy?)
     ↓
IV.  Venture Architecture  (What are we building?)
     ↓
V.   Execution             (How do we build it?)
     ↓
VI.  Evidence              (How do we prove it?)
```

## Phase I — Founder Identity

Documents: 01-03
Purpose: Define the founder. These survive all ventures.

| # | Document | Question |
|---|----------|----------|
| 01 | Founder Doctrine | What are my immutable convictions? What will I never sacrifice? |
| 02 | Founder Manifest | Who am I as a builder? Philosophy, approach, strengths, weaknesses. |
| 03 | Life Mission | What civilization-scale problem am I solving with my life? |

## Phase II — Civilization Thinking

Documents: 04-06
Purpose: Understand the world. Why this mission exists.

| # | Document | Question |
|---|----------|----------|
| 04 | Civilization Thesis | How does the world work? Why do some continents dominate? |
| 05 | Problem Thesis | What is THE root problem (not the symptom)? |
| 06 | Theory of Change | If X changes → Y changes → Z → mission accomplished. |

## Phase III — Strategic Architecture

Documents: 07-12
Purpose: Define the strategy. This is where the first venture appears.

| # | Document | Question |
|---|----------|----------|
| 07 | Grand Strategy | The 20-30 year plan. All ventures, labs, foundations. How everything works together. |
| 08 | Venture Selection | Why THIS venture? Why KORA before OMNI? Why not something else? |
| 09 | Vision | The future created. |
| 10 | Mission | The daily work. What we do. |
| 11 | Strategic Principles | Rules of execution. Excellence, focus, compounding. |
| 12 | North Star | The ultimate metric. The variable that represents progress. |

## Phase IV — Venture Architecture

Documents: 13-15
Purpose: Define what exactly is being built.

| # | Document | Question |
|---|----------|----------|
| 13 | Strategic Assets Map | What assets are needed? Models, compute, datasets, distribution, capital, talent. |
| 14 | Capability Map | What capabilities are needed? Research, training, inference, deployment, marketing. |
| 15 | Master Plan | How to assemble assets and capabilities. |

## Phase V — Execution

Documents: 16-18
Purpose: Define how to build it.

| # | Document | Question |
|---|----------|----------|
| 16 | Strategic Roadmap | Technical, product, and research roadmap. |
| 17 | Capital Roadmap | How much, when, why, in what form. |
| 18 | Business Plan | Document for partners, investors, institutions, compute providers, banks. |

## Phase VI — Evidence

Document: 19
Purpose: Prove everything.

| # | Document | Content |
|---|----------|---------|
| 19 | Annexes | Studies, benchmarks, calculations, architecture, budget, TRL, detailed roadmaps, research. |

## Legacy Documents

Previous VAOS versions had additional specialized documents (Revenue Architecture, GTM, DIOS, Organization Design, Team OS, Governance, Risk, Scenarios, Legacy, etc.). These remain valuable as reference annexes or sub-sections of Business Plan (doc 18) and Annexes (doc 19). They are not part of the core 19-doc cascade.

## Related Systems

- **DIOS** — Distribution. Connected at Venture Architecture phase.
- **CEOS** — Content production for the venture.
- **FAOS** — Fundraising and capital strategy.
- **CAOS** — Pricing and allocation.

## VAOS Workflow

```
STEP 0: Receive raw idea or existing venture
STEP 01-03: Load Founder Identity (Phase I)
STEP 04-06: Load Civilization Thinking (Phase II)
STEP 07-12: Load Strategic Architecture (Phase III)
STEP 13-15: Load Venture Architecture (Phase IV)
STEP 16-18: Load Execution (Phase V)
STEP 19: Load Evidence (Phase VI)
→ Hand off to FounderHQ operations
```

## Definition of Success

A successful VAOS execution produces a venture that:
1. Derives from a clear founder identity (not opportunistic)
2. Has a civilizational thesis (not just a product idea)
3. Has a testable theory of change
4. Has a strategy (not just a roadmap)
5. Has a concrete asset and capability map
6. Has a credible execution plan with capital requirements
7. Has evidence to prove it's possible
